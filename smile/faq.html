FAQ's
Is help only available online?

No. We reach out to individuals if we see it fit. We help individuals connect with the authorities in many unavoidable instances where loved ones are missing. We also provide one on one counselling sessions for those who have no will to live or have lost hope. For any details about individual reach-out, please call our toll-free number at the bottom of the page and follow the instructions.

Can I access information without internet?

Yes. You can sign up for updates via text messages. The frequency upon which these messages are sent is personal, you can choose a daily basis, once a week, once in 2 weeks or monthly. Text charges may be incurred. Those who have wifi can also download videos and watch at a future date.

Who can be helped?

Everyone who is willing to seek for help is welcome. We accommodate the young, old, sick, addicts, as long as you are searching for the lost or feel lonely, you are welcome.

Can I volunteer?

We believe in diversity of knowledge and are happy to have people from all walks of life help in this cause. Please signup early enough for your intended report day, for better preparation on our side. Where can I find more information about this organization? You can click on ABOUT US, at our home page. It has details of who we are, when we started, why we are in operation, our team members and the location of our offices.

Where are you located?

We have 30 branches in over 16 African countries, as you click on the ABOUT US icon you will be directed to a location in our country of operation.

Who can access the Blog?

It is for everyone to share their concerns and have the public give an opinion or a solution over it. The Replies will also be filtered so as to have only those which uplift and have clean language.

My situation is private, I don't want to post in public?

There are two options before you post, you can choose public or private. For private choices, our services are available 24/7 and someone will always be ready to render help.
